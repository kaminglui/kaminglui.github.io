Read this first

  1. What it does K-means partitions points into k clusters by alternating two moves: assign each point to the nearest centroid, then recompute each centroid as the mean of its members.
  2. Try this Click to drop ~20 points in a few visible groups, press Initialize centroids, then Step a few times to watch centroids march toward the groups they own.
  3. Look for this The SSE chip drops on every step until assignments stop changing — that's convergence. Different starting centroids can land on different local minima.

How to read what you're seeing

The canvas

Each dot is a data point

Points coloured the same belong to the same cluster. The larger filled shapes are the centroids — the current estimate of each cluster's centre.

The numbers

SSE is what we're minimising

Sum of Squared Errors = Σ ‖point − its centroid‖². K-means is guaranteed to reduce (or hold) SSE on every iteration, and stops when assignments don't change.

The catch

Initialisation matters

K-means converges to a local minimum. Try pressing Initialize centroids a few times on the overlapping preset — the final SSE often lands on different values.
